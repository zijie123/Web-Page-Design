In many places, the folk dwellings organize the architectural layout with courtyards and courtyards. The courtyards are places where people can move in the weather, while the courtyards are places where people can't move in the weather. The mainstream of traditional folk houses in various regions of China?

The mainstream of traditional dwellings in various regions of China is regular residence, which is typical of Beijing quadrangle courtyard which is arranged in a symmetrical way. Beijing Siheyuan is divided into two chambers, with the central principal room system being the most respected. It is the place for holding family etiquette and receiving distinguished guests. Each building is connected to the courtyard by verandas. Although Beijing siheyuan is the Chinese feudal society patriarchal patriarchal concept and the family system in the residential building on the concrete performance, but the courtyard square is wide, the scale is appropriate, quiet kind, flowers and trees in order, it is a very ideal outdoor living space.

What is the main form of Chinese traditional residence?

Wooden frame courtyard type, its number is large, the distribution is wide, for the Han, Manchu, Bai and other ethnic groups in the majority of people and a part of the use of other minorities. This kind of residence is mainly made of wood frame housing, which is built along the main axis of the north-south main hall or principal room. In front of the principal room, the east and west wing rooms are built around the confrontation. This kind of distribution is in line with the Chinese feudal society in family life to distinguish between senior and junior, inside and outside the requirements of etiquette. Among them, the quadrangle courtyard is represented by the quadrangle courtyard in Beijing, forming a unique architectural style.

The Influence of Natural Environment on Dwellings in Ningxia?

Ningxia is located in the northwest far away from the sea, with less precipitation, large temperature difference, cold climate, obvious continental climate characteristics, dry winter and spring windy sand, prevailing northerly wind, so the residential buildings generally do not open north Windows. In order to keep warm and cold, it adopts the form of wing room and courtyard, and the house is compact. The roof form is one slope and two slopes coexist.

The characteristics of Chaoshan folk houses?

Chaozhou folk house is a traditional folk house building in Chaoshan area of Guangdong Province, with distinctive characteristics and high aesthetic value. Chaoshan ethnic group and live, along the river coastal plain area, the economy is more developed, many prominent families live in these places, the old county seat is also more than a group of ethnic group and live in the situation. Therefore, both urban and rural residents have a strong clan concept, which is reflected in the settlement architecture which is a walled village pattern centered on the family name and ancestral hall. Chaoshan rural communities and live, the whole village, are based on the idea of their clans, geomantic concepts, production and life, defense functions and some aesthetic concepts to build.

What are the two systems of Chinese traditional architecture?

official and folk. Official buildings, such as palaces, shrines, mausoleums, temples, mansions, etc., and folk buildings, such as dwellings, gardens, ancestral halls, guilds, etc. Dwelling houses, as one of the contents of traditional architecture, have obvious local characteristics and strong ethnic characteristics because of their wide distribution, large quantity and close correlation with the life and production of the people of various nationalities. From the historical practice of the nation, it summarizes its excellent characteristics and experience, which are also used for reference and application in today's architectural creation.

How does the dwelling house reveal the national characteristics?

Residential buildings do not have a set of procedural rules and regulations and practices like official buildings. They can build houses according to local natural conditions, their own economic level and the characteristics of building materials. The characteristics of folk dwellings mainly come from the national living customs, production mode, religious belief, psychological hobby and aesthetic concept, while the experience of folk dwellings comes from the local natural environment and climatic and geographical conditions. These two are inseparable, are closely related, they together constitute the national cultural characteristics and local characteristics of dwellings.

stilted building

Stilted buildings are traditional dwellings of Miao, Zhuang, Buyi and other nationalities. The basic characteristics of stilted buildings are that the main house is built on the ground, and the wing room is usually partially suspended and supported by pillars, which belongs to the semi-dry column architecture,

Distribution range: southeast of Guizhou, west of Hunan, north of Guangxi, west of Hubei, etc.

The natural environment: mainly related to rugged mountainous terrain, small building area, in addition to play a role in ventilation and drying, guard against wild animals and poisonous snakes.

Dai bamboo house is typical of the inherent of the building, is the upper and lower layer of tall buildings. The bottom floor is generally not human, used for raising livestock, poultry, the upper floor for people to live, belongs to the dry column type building.

Distribution range: Yunnan, mainly in Xishuangbanna.

The relationship between high feet and natural environment: high feet are designed to block the moisture on the ground and facilitate air circulation to ventilate and dehumidify, so as to adapt to the high temperature and rainy climate. In addition, it is simple and easy to build, and can avoid floods and insects and snakes.

Kaiping Diaolou

Kaiping Diaolou is located in Kaiping City, Guangdong Province. It is a multi-story tower building integrating defense and residence.

Cultural implication/geographical background: Kaiping is located at the junction of four counties. In ancient times, the management of Kaiping was chaotic, bandits were rampant, there were many rivers, and flood disasters were frequent. Local people were forced to build diaotou in the village to protect themselves. In addition, there are a large number of overseas Chinese living in this area. Diaolou is mostly the product of the integration of western architectural art and local architectural art.

Yikeyin dwellings

The Yikeyin dwellings were jointly created by the Han and Yi ancestors and first emerged in Kunming area. The basic pattern is "three rooms and two ears inverted eight feet", and the plane is nearly square.

Cultural connotation: It began to evolve on the basis of the architecture of the Han Siheyuan, and gradually formed the basic pattern and shape of "three rooms, four ears and eight feet" in the early Qing Dynasty.
