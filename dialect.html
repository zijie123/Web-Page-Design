Chinese dialects

There are many factors in dialect formation, including social, historical and geographical factors, such as long-term small-scale agricultural economy, social division, migration of population, and barrier of mountains and rivers; There are also factors that belong to the language itself, such as the imbalance of language development, the conflict and influence between different languages. Modern Chinese has different dialects, and they are widely distributed in different areas.

Dialectal variation

The differences among modern Chinese dialects are manifested in phonetics, vocabulary and grammar, especially in phonetics.

According to dialects, Chinese can be divided into seven categories: Mandarin dialects and other six southern dialects. Corresponding to Guangyun, it belongs to the descendants of middle ancient Chinese

Development history

Chinese linguists have been arguing about the division of Chinese dialects. Some people divide Chinese into seven dialects, some into five dialects, others into six dialects, eight dialects and even ten dialects. But what we all agree with is that no matter what kind of division method is adopted, the internal users of these "big dialects" sometimes can't understand each other. In different dialect areas, people's dialect consciousness is also different. Chinese dialects can also be divided into many sub dialects, which can be subdivided into a number of small pieces and dialect points.
